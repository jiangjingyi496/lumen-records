Light, O Light, from whence you came,
光明啊，光明，你从何而来
Across the sea, across the flame,
越过海洋，越过火焰
You touch the brow of every land,
你轻抚每一片土地的额头
And wake the eyes that watching stand.
唤醒那守望的双眼

For seventy winters I have prayed,
七十年来，寒冬我祈求
For seventy springs my lamp I stayed;
七十春来，独守烛影
Through every night I kept the flame,
每夜我将灯火长明
Lest cold should fall upon your name.
唯恐寒意侵扰你的芳名

I did not wait for word alone,
我未曾期盼着只言片语
Nor altar-fire, nor carven stone;
也未祈求圣火或石像
I waited, Lord, to see your face
主啊，我在此地等候
Within the living, in this place.
只为一睹您在生者中的容颜

Light, come down into our night,
光明，请降临我们的夜晚
Light, we kindle lamps so bright.
光明，我们点亮如此明亮的灯火
Light, come home to those you own,
光明，请回到属于你的地方
Dwell in flesh, no more alone.
栖息于躯体之中，不再孤单

No city's wall, no sword, no throne,
没有城市的高墙，没有利剑，没有王座
No frozen law in silent stone —
没有冰冷的法律镌刻在静默的石碑上——
But holiness in mortal breath,
但有凡人气息中的神圣
A living word to conquer death.
有鲜活的话语，能战胜死亡

The day the law would learn to burn,
当法律学会燃烧的那一天
The day the stone would beat and turn,
当石头会敲击并改变一切的那一天
The day the word would wear our skin —
当话语会磨蚀我们的肌肤的那一天——
For this, for this, I watched within.
为了这一切，为了这一切，我曾默默注视着这一切
